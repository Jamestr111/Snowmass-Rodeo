Our story

52 years of Wednesday nights — the people who started it, the family who runs it now, and the community that shows up.

History

Built in a summer, running for 52 years.

In 1972, a local competitive roper and outfitter named Doug McLain set out to build a weekly rodeo in Snowmass. With about $1,200 and help from friends, he built the arena and bleachers himself. The following summer, roughly 1,400 people showed up for the very first Snowmass Rodeo on June 13, 1973 — before Snowmass Village was even officially an incorporated town.

It sold out then, and it still sells out now. In 2003, a group of Snowmass residents formalized that community effort into a nonprofit — the Snowmass Western Heritage Association — to keep the rodeo, and the western heritage behind it, going for the long run. The rodeo paused for one summer during the COVID-19 pandemic, which pushed its 50th-anniversary season to 2024. 2026 marks the 52nd season.

Still one of the best in the country

Snowmass Rodeo is one of the longest-running rodeos in Colorado, and has repeatedly landed on USA Today's 10 Best Rodeos list nationally.

The People

Who built it, who runs it

A handful of names show up again and again across five decades of Snowmass Rodeo.

Founder

Doug McLain

Built the original arena and bleachers by hand in 1972 and ran the very first rodeo in 1973. Still roping in the arena decades later.

Original Announcer

Twirp Anderson

Co-created the rodeo alongside McLain and called the action from the announcer's booth for 30 years.

Arena Director

Jim Snyder

A longtime local ranch manager, Jim has served as arena director and SWHA board president, keeping the rodeo's western roots intact as ranching itself has modernized.

Executive Director

Darce Vold

A third-generation stock contractor and owner of Triple V Rodeo Company, Darce has produced the Wednesday night rodeo and led SWHA as executive director since 2013.

Get Involved

Volunteer at the rodeo

From gate crews to the chutes to the ticket booth, Snowmass Rodeo runs on volunteers every single Wednesday. Volunteers help with parking and shuttles, kids' event sign-ups, the chutes and arena crew, and the gate. No experience needed — just show up ready to work.

In Memory

Remembering our rodeo family

Over 52 years, Snowmass Rodeo has lost dear friends, competitors, volunteers, and board members who helped build what this rodeo is today. We hold their memory close.

In Memory of Markey Butler

This year, Snowmass Rodeo remembers Markey Butler — a former mayor of Snowmass Village and a dedicated SWHA board member and volunteer. As mayor, she was a steady, reliable supporter of the rodeo, and she understood it as more than a summer event: it was tradition, community, and family.
