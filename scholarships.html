Scholarships

Every Wednesday night keeps this rodeo running — and helps send its next generation of volunteers and contestants to college.

How It Works

Show up for the season, earn toward your education.

The Snowmass Western Heritage Association has built its scholarship program around the same thing that built the rodeo: showing up, week after week. Volunteers and contestants who work or compete at seven of the season's ten Wednesday night rodeos become eligible for an award toward their education — up to $5,000.

Up to $5,000

Awarded per qualifying student, toward tuition and continuing education.

7 of 10 Rodeos

The season's attendance bar for volunteers and contestants to qualify.

Volunteers & Contestants

Open to the young people who work and compete at the rodeo all summer.

Funded By You

Every ticket, sponsorship, and donation feeds the fund directly.

Where It Leads

Rodeo kids grow up to be everything.

Past recipients have gone on to study law, medicine, environmental science, politics, and veterinary medicine, among other fields. Different paths, same starting point — a summer spent showing up at the arena.

It's a small-town version of a simple idea: the community that raises you can also help send you to school.

Applications open each year on our official application page. See eligibility details and deadlines there.
